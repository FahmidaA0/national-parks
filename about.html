Public Parks Shouldn't Pay the Price

We're fighting against the privatization and defunding of New York's public green spaces

Here's The Problem

New York City's parks are facing a growing crisis of inequality. While wealthy neighborhoods enjoy beautifully maintained parks through private funding, low-income communities watch their green spaces crumble due to budget cuts.

78% of park maintenance budgets come from private sources in wealthy areas

Canarsie Park

How This Happened

  1. 1980s: City begins cutting parks department budgets
  2. 1990s: Wealthy neighborhoods form private conservancies
  3. 2008: Financial crisis leads to more budget cuts
  4. Today: 80% of private park funding goes to just 5% of parks
